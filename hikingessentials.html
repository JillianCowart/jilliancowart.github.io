10 Hiking Essentials

#1 NAVIGATION

Bring a compass, topographical map, or GPS to plan out and navigate your trail.

#2 MEDICAL SUPPLIES

Prepare for medical emergencies by packing first-aid supplies. Make sure to replace expired materials.

#3 HYDRATION

Physical activity can cause dehydration. Bring water treatment supplies and plenty of water to stay hydrated.

#4 FOOD

Prepare for unexpected trip changes by packing an extra day's worth of nutritional food that does not require cooking.

#5 LIGHT

Flashlights, lanterns, and headlamps are good light sources to prepare for traveling in the dark. Make sure to pack extra batteries.

#6 FIRE SOURCES

Pack matches and fire starters in case an emergency signal, staying warm, or cooking heat source is needed. Be familiar with park fire regulations.

#7 TOOLS

Bring a repair kit and protection gear such as duct tape, a knife, scissors, and animal spray for emergencies, specific trip activities, and protection.

#8 CLOTHING

Prepare for unpredictable weather by packing extra clothing that is appropriate for extreme conditions.

#9 SHELTER

Shelter is important for protection from weather in emergency survival situations. Light weight options include a tent, tarp, hammock, or space blanket.

#10 SUN PROTECTION

Sunglasses, hats, or sunscreen can help protect your skin and eyes from harmful UV rays that can increase your risk of sunburns and skin cancers.
